# Pakistan Economic Indicators Analysis 🇵🇰

A comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on World Bank data containing over 1,400 economic indicators for Pakistan from 1960 to 2024. This project examines how key metrics including GDP growth, inflation, remittances, urbanization, and population have shaped Pakistan's economic trajectory over six decades.

---

## Dataset

- **Source:** [World Bank Open Data](https://data.worldbank.org/country/pakistan)
- **Coverage:** 1960 – 2024
- **Indicators Used:**
  - GDP Growth
  - GDP Per Capita
  - Inflation
  - Population
  - Life Expectancy
  - Remittances
  - Unemployment
  - Mobile Subscriptions
  - Urban Population

---

## 🎯 Objective

The main objective of this project is to analyze Pakistan’s long-term economic development using real-world World Bank data and uncover meaningful trends, correlations, and economic patterns through data science techniques and visualizations.

This project aims to:

- Clean and preprocess large-scale real-world economic data
- Identify major economic trends from 1960–2024
- Analyze relationships between indicators such as GDP growth, inflation, population, and urbanization
- Detect economic anomalies and inflation spikes
- Visualize insights using Python and Matplotlib

## 🔍 Key Findings
- Pakistan recorded its highest GDP growth in 1970 at over 10%, but long-term trends show increasing volatility and slower growth in recent decades.
- Highest inflation was recorded in 2023 at 30.7%, with major spikes in 1974, 2008, and 2023 driven by oil shocks, global financial crisis, and political instability.
- Population is growing rapidly while urbanization increases slowly, indicating rural population is expanding faster than urban areas.
- Rising inflation during periods of weak GDP growth suggests stagflation-like pressures in Pakistan's economy.
- GDP per capita strongly correlates with mobile subscriptions, reflecting that economic growth drives technology adoption.
- Remittances increase alongside unemployment, indicating more Pakistanis seek overseas employment as domestic opportunities shrink.
